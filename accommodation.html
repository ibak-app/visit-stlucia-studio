Choose Your Area

Where you stay shapes your entire trip. Saint Lucia has three main accommodation clusters ‚Äî each with a very different feel.

Rodney Bay / Cap Estate

The north ‚Äî lively, convenient, beach bars, restaurants, marina, water sports. Best for first-timers, couples who want nightlife options, and those on package holidays. Closest to George F.L. Charles Airport (regional).

Soufriere / Piton Area

The southwest ‚Äî dramatic, remote, volcanic. The Pitons, Sulphur Springs, Anse Chastanet and Diamond Falls are all here. Best for nature lovers, honeymoons, and travellers who want Saint Lucia's most spectacular scenery. 1‚Äì1.5 hours from Hewanorra Airport.

Marigot Bay

Mid-island, hidden gem. A stunning natural harbour with a romantic village atmosphere. Best for sailing enthusiasts, couples seeking seclusion, and those who want to explore both north and south easily. Limited dining options nearby.

Which Airport?

Most international flights land at Hewanorra (UVF) in the south. The scenic drive north to Rodney Bay takes 1.5‚Äì2 hours on winding mountain roads. Staying in Soufriere or Marigot Bay makes much more sense if you land at UVF. George F.L. Charles Airport (SLU) in Castries handles regional hops and is much closer to Rodney Bay.

Budget Accommodation

Saint Lucia is a more expensive destination than some Caribbean islands, but there are genuinely affordable options if you know where to look.

Saint Lucia has a good network of family-run guesthouses, particularly in Castries, Gros Islet and Vieux Fort. These offer clean, simple rooms with breakfast sometimes included. Service is personal and authentic ‚Äî you'll get genuine local recommendations rather than tourist-industry advice.

Find them via: Booking.com (filter by price), local Facebook groups, or ask at the Tourist Board office. Word of mouth is powerful here.

Saint Lucia has a good Airbnb supply, particularly around Rodney Bay, Cap Estate and Marigot Bay. Private villas with pools are available from US$200‚Äì500/night ‚Äî often better value than luxury hotels when shared by a group or family. Airbnb is often the best option for stays of a week or more.

Look for: Properties with kitchens (save on food costs), those with pools (you won't need to pay for resort day passes), and anything with direct beach access.

Saint Lucia isn't a big backpacker destination and doesn't have many traditional hostels. Your best budget options are guesthouses and Airbnb rooms. Gros Islet village has some of the cheapest accommodation on the island and is very well-positioned for Rodney Bay activities.

Budget tips:
‚Äî Eat at local village restaurants and rum shops (EC$15‚Äì30 per meal)
‚Äî Use minibuses instead of taxis (EC$2‚Äì5 per trip vs US$15‚Äì30)
‚Äî Cook in your accommodation when possible
‚Äî Visit beaches and hiking trails directly rather than paying for resort access

Booking Tips

Peak season (December‚ÄìApril): Book 3‚Äì6 months in advance. Christmas and New Year are fully booked at the best properties 6‚Äì12 months ahead. Prices are 30‚Äì50% higher than low season.

Low season (May‚ÄìNovember): Better availability, significantly lower prices. Last-minute deals are possible. The Jazz Festival week (May) and Carnival (July) are exceptions ‚Äî book well ahead for those specific dates.

Book direct with the hotel when possible ‚Äî they often match or beat online travel agents and may add extras (room upgrade, late checkout, breakfast) to secure the booking. Many Saint Lucia properties have a "best rate guarantee" if you call directly.

For package holidays (flight + hotel), comparison sites like Expedia, Hotels.com and Booking.com can be excellent value. Sandals has its own booking system.

Always check what's included in quoted rates. Hotels in Saint Lucia typically add:

  • Room tax: 10% on hotel accommodation
  • Service charge: 10% (most properties)
  • Environmental levy: Small per-night charge

The total can add 20‚Äì22% to the room rate. Better to book with taxes included and get a clear final price upfront.

Consider Renting a Car

If you're staying in an independent hotel (not all-inclusive), renting a car makes a huge difference to your experience. Saint Lucia's best beaches, restaurants and attractions are spread across the island. Taxi costs add up quickly ‚Äî a car rental at US$40‚Äì70/day often works out cheaper than daily taxi use for an active traveller.
