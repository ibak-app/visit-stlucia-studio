Street Food & The Fish Fry

Saint Lucia's two Friday evening street food events are unmissable experiences and the best value eating on the island.

Anse la Raye Friday Night Fish Fry

Every Friday from around 6pm, the waterfront road in Anse la Raye fishing village closes to traffic and fills with food stalls, smoke and music. Fresh fish, lobster, lambi (conch), fried bakes and local provisions â€” all cooked over coal fires and served at prices locals actually pay. A genuine community celebration that tourists are warmly welcomed into.

  • Location: Anse la Raye village, ~25 mins south of Rodney Bay
  • Time: Friday evenings from ~6pm until late
  • Budget: EC$40â€“120 for a full meal with drinks

Gros Islet Jump Up (Street Party)

The Gros Islet Street Party on Friday nights is another beloved Saint Lucia tradition. The main street of Gros Islet village fills with food, drink, music and dancing. More of a party atmosphere than the fish fry, but with the same local food vendors. Easier to reach from Rodney Bay (10-minute drive or EC$10â€“15 taxi).

  • Location: Gros Islet village, near Rodney Bay
  • Time: Friday evenings from ~9pm onwards
  • Vibe: Louder and more party-focused than Anse la Raye
