Why Saint Lucia?

A record-breaking year for tourism, a new Olympic champion, and one of the Caribbean's most dramatic landscapes ‚Äî Saint Lucia is having a moment.

2024 Olympic Gold

Saint Lucia's own Julien Alfred won Olympic gold in the 100m at Paris 2024 ‚Äî the island's first ever Olympic medal and a moment of national pride that you'll still feel when you visit.

Saint Lucia is a small island with an outsized personality. The 238-square-mile island packs in dramatic volcanic scenery, the kind of beaches that make screensavers look ordinary, world-class food, and genuine Caribbean warmth. It's the only country in the world named after a woman, and it has the rare distinction of producing two Nobel Prize winners ‚Äî the most per capita of any nation.

The 2024 stayover arrivals figure of 435,959 is a record high ‚Äî proof that travellers who discover Saint Lucia tend to come back, and word is spreading fast. The ongoing US$157‚Äì175 million expansion of Hewanorra International Airport will make getting here even easier in the coming years.

Officially hurricane season, but Saint Lucia sits south enough to avoid most storms. This period brings occasional short-burst rain showers (often in the afternoon), lush green landscapes, and significantly lower prices.

The real story: Most days in the low season are perfectly sunny and enjoyable. Rainfall is usually brief and the island turns an extraordinary shade of green. You'll often have beaches to yourself.
