Culture & History

Fourteen changes of colonial rule between France and Britain left Saint Lucia with one of the Caribbean's most complex and fascinating cultural identities.

The bustling heart of Castries, the capital. The covered market sells fresh produce, spices, hot sauces, local craft, clothing and street food. Saturday morning is the busiest and best time to visit when farmers come in from across the island.

Location: Corner of Jeremie & Peynier Streets, Castries.
Best for: Buying local spices, hot pepper sauce, and watching Saint Lucia's daily life unfold. Very affordable â€” this is where locals shop.

"The Hill of Good Luck" has witnessed 14 battles between the French and British. Today, Fort Charlotte at the summit offers panoramic views over Castries harbour and the northwest coast. The fort itself is partly occupied by a community college but the grounds and ramparts are open to walk.

The view from here at sunset rivals anything in the Caribbean for sheer drama â€” city, harbour, mountains, and sea.

Saint Lucia's own Bounty Rum is distilled at the St. Lucia Distillers Group near Roseau. The distillery tour takes you through the entire production process â€” from fermented molasses to barrel ageing â€” with a generous tasting at the end. Chairman's Reserve and Bounty are the flagship brands.

St. Lucia Distillers: Roseau Valley. Tours ~US$25â€“35. Book in advance.
Also try: 1931 Rum and Admiral Rodney Reserve â€” premium aged expressions.

A 250-year-old estate near Soufriere that gives you a genuine taste of Saint Lucia's plantation history â€” including its difficult colonial past. The grounds are stunning: working cacao, banana and coconut plantations, restored colonial buildings, and exceptional Creole cuisine at the restaurant.

Avoid Touts at the Port

If you arrive by cruise ship, you'll be approached immediately by unofficial tour guides. These can be fine, but you'll get much better value by booking with established operators in advance, or by asking your hotel or the Tourist Board office for recommendations. Always agree on a price before you get in a taxi or commit to a tour.
